# 👋

## Music
My relationship to music has changed in the last three months. It's like I suddenly remembered just how music used to mean to me. Like that idea slipped from my head in the last decade So far I think this is the biggest change.
I unsubscribed from Spotify Premium. I exported my data into an Excel spreadsheet. 
I created a Bandcamp account. I started following artists I like and subscribed to their email newsletters for updates.

I started following music sites again, with a special shout-out to Exclaim.ca, a Canadian music and film magazine. I have a few others on my list that I need to check out, like earshot-online.com, a kind of gathering of university campus radio stations from across Canada. If anyone sees this and wants to recommend Canadian music blogs, don't hesitate. 

I'm also looking more at themusic labels some of my favourite artists are signed to and learning about other artists that I may like that they also signed.

I absolutely want to buy a DAC (Digital Audio Player) - a music player. Like an iPod or an "mp3 player." Except it plays more than mp3. That way I can download a music library organizer, create my own playlists and curate my music. Then I can keep purchasing digital files from Bandcamp, CDs from record stores, and music directly from the artists themselves. Many of these players also support audiobooks and podcasts, via RSS capture. 

One pain point about this approach is commuting. I use android auto extensively. For music, navigation, podcasts, audiobooks. Everything is there. Could I use a DAC and also navigate?
