## The Q is for Qurating
This isn't what I wanted. I started this blog to write more, because I missed that part of myself. I wanted to try to get back pieces of me that have been swept under a rug for too long. I've made some progress, but not enough.

## In the weeds
I'm tech-adjacent. As an elder millennial I've loved fixing computers and taking things apart,. I learned the basics of coding but never make a career out of it. When I learned about Hugo I loved the philosophy behind it, and compared to other options it was affordable so I dug in. But then I got into the weeds of trying to customize my blog the way I wanted with themes and custom CSS, and using Obsidian to write in... honestly it's not unlike the time I used to spend designing my MySpace profile with HTML. Except now I have two kids and responsibilities and very little time. Even now I'm stealing time by typing on laptop while the kids destroy the living room.  Oh, Happy Father's day btw.

## Music update
I haven't bought an iPod replacement yet. BUT, I have by going to shops  to buy used CDs. I've started organizing my music with Jellyfin and Finamp. Finamp is so awesome. Jellyfin is just as awesome. 

This week I burned a few CDs for the first time in like twenty years? Curating CD playlists was a huge part of my twenties. I put a lot of time and thought into so many of them, I miss that level of engagement with music. I bought a CD case to keep in the car. Now I listen to entire albums, as they were designed to be listened to, sometimes over and over again.

Not all the songs are my favourite, but  I still listen to them and try to get something out of them.

## Reading
I'm reading a lot more. I track my reading on Storygraph, which, unlike Goodreads, *isn't* owned by Amazon and *isn't* gathering data to use against you.

## Phone Use
I got interrupted in the [Q1 post]({{< relref "posts/Q1 Update" >}}) and didn't get to finish this section. I was doing really well with my phone use... and then I put my house up for sale. That meant keeping my phone on me basically all the time, whether to communicate with my realtor who lives on his phone, or mortgage rep, or look tirelessly at any new house listing in case it might be the one to put an offer on. (It was slim pickings out there.) So I'm back in the habit now, and I'll have to break it again.

But, I am still off Instagram, and really happy about that. The Never Post podcast team had a good analogy for it (I think it was Mike that mentioned it?) - Instagram is just a mall now. And to put my own spin on it, it's one of those malls that was losing a ton of traffic, so they retrofitted it to be high end, and now the stores are mostly too expensive or at least feel like they are, so people just walk up and down the floorplan like zombies until they feel depressed and go home. Instagram isn't about learning what your friends are doing, it isn't about posting what's interesting in your own life, it's just free samples of perfumes and candies.

Here's hoping Bubbles continues to feel fun and fresh.
